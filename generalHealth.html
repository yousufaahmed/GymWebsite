Exercise:

Exercise is very important in our lives, because it keeps our brain, mind, and body healthy. It gives your body strength and stamina, which helps reduce damage to your body in ways such as strain and cramps, but it also makes your body used to things like running, which means that all of your muscles will work better when you get older. If you go and have a look at an old age home, almost all the old people over there can barely move. This is mainly because of their lack of exercise when growing up or when they were adults. If you want to protect yourself from becoming defenceless when you grow up, then exercise is the way to go. Exercise also makes it so that you are less likely to get ill, because when you are training yourself out in the gym, you are also training your immune system to fight all the bacteria and viruses that enter your body.

Healthiness isn't all about exercise, though. You have to eat healthily too. Head on down to the diet page to check out the best meals for your exercising preference. If you want to know more about exercises to do, then check out the Teen and Adult pages, for exercises which will make you a healthier, stronger person. Common Questions:

How important is exercise?

It is probably the most important thing on earth after eating and drinking. It keeps you healthy, medically and physically.

What is exercise?

Exercise is a series of repetetive actions which train your body to have more stamina and strength.

Is exercising dangerous?

Exercising could be dangerous if not done properly, but our staff always supervise, and help out if you are in trouble. You can ask for a trainer at the reception if you want to.

Why is healthy eating important?

Exercising is useless without healthy eating, as you won't lose any weight if you dont eat healthy. Some exercise routines also require a special diet, such as a protein diet for muscle exercises.
